Phase 10 — Built for Volume, Learnable in a Day

Five changes from the evaluation, ordered by leverage. The first two attack both weaknesses at once (scale and intuitiveness); the rest go one level deeper. Each step ships and stops for your review, as always. The tenancy layer stays untouched — it's right.

10.1

Global search that actually works

effort M · the single biggest fix for both scale and intuitiveness
Why first: at 100K claims, search is how anyone finds anything — and today the box is painted on. It's also the intuitiveness fix: a new rep who can type a patient name and land on the right claim doesn't need to understand the nav first.
  • One shared component (search web component, like the practice/role switchers) dropped into the header of every billing screen — build once, wire everywhere.
  • Searches everything in the stores: patients, claim IDs, payers, CPT codes, facilities, payer references (EFT/check traces), decision titles.
  • Grouped results panel with keyboard navigation: Claims · Patients · Payers & Contracts · Payments · Decisions — each row deep-links to the right screen (claim → Claim Detail, payer → Contracts, trace → Payments deposits).
  • Scale behaviors baked in: result caps with "show all N in Register" overflow rows that land on the Register pre-filtered — search and the Register learn to work together.
10.2

Agent Inbox at volume — grouping & batch approval

effort M · the exception queue has to scale too
Why second: exception-based supervision only works if the exceptions themselves stay manageable. At hundreds of decisions/day, a flat card list becomes the new backlog.
  • Group-by-type view (default above ~10 open decisions): one row per decision archetype — "Small-balance write-offs (38) · $412 total · all within your threshold" — expandable to the individual cards.
  • Batch approve on uniform groups: where every member passes the same policy check, one action approves the group; mixed groups force item-by-item. The distinction itself is governance — the agent states why a group is or isn't batchable.
  • Simulated volume day: a "busy morning" dataset (~60 decisions) behind a toggle, so the demo can show the inbox surviving scale, not just a quiet Tuesday.
  • Routing rule stated in the UI: a one-line header — "Every approval lives here; queues on Payments/Worklist are working views of the same decisions" — kills the two-front-doors confusion.
10.3

Claims Register — saved views & bulk actions

effort M
  • Saved views: current filter combination saves under a name ("Secondary denials > $200"), pinned as chips above the table, persisted per person. Ships with 3 starter views.
  • Bulk actions: checkbox column + action bar on selection — assign to rep, add to worklist, export CSV, request write-off batch. Governed actions route through the Inbox, not around it.
  • Register receives search overflow from 10.1 (pre-filtered deep links), making it the "show me all N" surface.
10.4

First-day experience — orientation & glossary

effort S–M · makes "learnable in a day" literal
  • "How this portal thinks" panel: a dismissible first-run card on Billing Home — five sentences: the agent works, you decide, everything lands in the Inbox, balances are owned until zero, autonomy is governed. The mental model in 30 seconds.
  • Inline glossary: one shared term component — dotted underline under 835, PLB, CO-45, MA18, 271, COB, ERA; hover/tap shows a plain-language definition. Applied to the highest-traffic screens (Inbox, Payments, Claim Detail).
  • Zero states with intent: empty Inbox and clean variance queue get designed states that say what the system is doing ("All clear — the agent is watching 4 crossovers and 2 veto windows"), not blank panels. The clean morning teaches the product.
10.5

Contracts at hundreds of payers

effort M
  • Search-first rate lookup: type-ahead across the full CPT catalog (simulated at a few hundred codes) replacing the 10-chip row; chips remain as "this practice's top codes" shortcuts.
  • Payer list built for 200: search + status filters (drift / missing codes / inferred-only / expiring soon), sorted exceptions-first, with a "needs attention" strip replacing the flat 8-row table as the default view.
  • Bulk exhibit import: the upload modal grows a multi-payer CSV path with a preview-and-confirm step — 40 exhibits land in one action, versioned and attributed like single loads.
Explicitly not in this phase Tenancy changes (the Ops layer is right) · per-practice contract scoping (worth a future phase if the demo needs it) · nav restructuring (10.1's search + 10.2's routing rule should relieve the 9-item nav; restructure only if they don't).
