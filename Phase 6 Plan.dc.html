2 · Phase 6a — Payer Rulebook

The compounding asset made visible and governable. Every rule the agent has learned — per payer, per practice — with provenance and controls.

Rulebook — new top-level screen Rulebook.dc.html
  • Rules table scoped by payer × practice: the rule, category (coding, COB, auth, enrollment…), status (Proposed / Active / Retired), hit count, $ prevented since adoption.
  • Provenance panel per rule: the denials that taught it (linked to Claim Detail), who approved it, when it last fired.
  • Manager actions: approve a proposed rule, edit its condition, retire it. Proposed rules also surface in the Agent Inbox as a task type, so approval happens where supervision already lives.
  • Example seeded from the AR report: the MHS HIP coding rule (84 denials, $11.8K) shown as the flagship Active rule.

Follow-on edits: rule chips on AR Worklist denials and Coverage checks become links into the Rulebook; Analytics "rules learned" tile links here; Rulebook added to side nav.

3 · Phase 6b — Agent QA & autonomy evidence

Today humans see only what the agent escalates. QA adds systematic spot-checks of what it didn't escalate — the evidence a manager needs to raise autonomy tiers with confidence.

QA tab inside Agent Inbox Agent Inbox v3.dc.html — new tab
  • Sample queue: a random N% of auto-approved actions per tier per week, presented for agree/disagree review with the same detail panel supervision already uses.
  • Agent scorecard: agreement rate by action type and tier, trend over time, disagreements linked to the action.
  • Tier recommendation: when agreement on a tier-gated action type stays above threshold for N weeks, the system proposes raising the tier — manager approves in one click; the change lands in the Audit Log.

4 · Phase 6c — Practice window

Closes PRD §10. The practice is the ultimate customer; the "weekly report relationship" moves into the product as a read-only live view — and the queue of things billing needs from the practice (insurance cards, COB forms, redetermination assists) becomes actionable instead of an email thread.

Billing Status — read-only view in the Clerk Portal Practice Billing View.dc.html
  • AR summary for the practice: collected MTD, balance by aging bucket, claims in flight — the weekly report, live.
  • Denials prevented this month (the Coverage layer's value, made visible to the customer who pays for it).
  • Action queue: "billing needs from you" — each Coverage exception's needs line routed to the practice with a done/attach flow. This is the piece that shortens every timeline on the Coverage board.

Open question folded in: this answers "do clerks keep a billing view" with yes, read-only + action queue. Confirm that's your intent before build.

5 · Phase 7 — Payment posting & reconciliation

The daily-bread workflow the portal currently skips. Agent auto-posts clean 835s; humans handle variances — same supervision model as everywhere else.

Payments — new top-level screen Payments.dc.html
  • Deposits view: ERA batches vs. bank deposits, matched / unmatched, by practice.
  • Variance queue: paid ≠ expected (allowed-amount variances, takebacks, interest) — agent proposes disposition (post, appeal, write off), manager approves above a $ threshold.
  • Write-off governance: approval thresholds per company in Settings; every write-off in the Audit Log.

6 · Phase 8 — Secondary billing & patient responsibility

The AR report's 409 "normal outcome" claims ($17.0K) moved to secondary or patient — today the portal loses sight of them there. Phase 8 keeps the balance owned until it's zero.

Extends Claims Register, Claim Detail, AR Worklist no new top-level screen

  • Claim Detail timeline extends through secondary adjudication; balance ledger shows primary → secondary → patient movement.
