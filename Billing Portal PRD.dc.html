PRD: Billing Portal & Billing Administration

A standalone, external-facing portal for revenue-cycle billing companies, plus the administrative surfaces to onboard and govern them — replacing Billing-inside-the-Clerk-Portal.

1 · Background & problem

The billing workspace (Dashboard, Agent Inbox, AR Worklist, Claims, Analytics) was prototyped as a subtab group inside the Clerk Portal. Its users, however, are external billing companies (e.g. Bell Medics working the practice's AR). Housing external users in an internal staff portal creates two structural problems:

  • Access boundary. Clerk Portal exposes Schedules, Calendar, Workflows, and the staff Directory. Hiding nav items is not a security boundary; an external party must log into a surface where everything visible is theirs to see.
  • Scale. Otangeles will serve multiple billing companies across multiple practices. Each company must see only the practices it is contracted for and only its own team's work — a tenant boundary, consistent with how the platform already isolates medical practices.

The platform already runs five portals on one shell (Platform Admin, Operations Admin, Provider, Scribe, Clerk). This PRD adds the sixth — the Billing Portal — and the admin surfaces that govern it.

2 · Goals & non-goals

Goals

  • A standalone Billing Portal, on the standard Otangeles portal shell, whose entire surface is billing work — nothing clinical, nothing scheduling, no staff directory.
  • Multi-tenant from day one: billing company ⟷ practice is many-to-many; every screen is scoped by (billing company, selected practice).
  • Billing companies manage their own teams (invite reps, set roles) so Otangeles ops does not administer every rep account.
  • Onboarding and governance of billing companies lives in Operations Admin, mirroring how medical practices are onboarded today.
  • Platform Admin sees billing roles, activity, and audit events like any other portal population.
  • All existing prototype functionality (priority worklist, agent inbox, claims timeline, analytics, capture workflows) re-homes without regression.

Non-goals (this phase)

  • Real authentication/credentials (per direction Jul 8 — design the boundary and flows, not auth plumbing).
  • Patient-responsibility collections and secondary billing workflows.
  • Payer call recording / audio capture (compliance scope).
  • Rebuilding the Clerk Portal; it simply loses the billing work surface. Whether clerks keep a read-only billing status view is an open question (§10).

3 · Personas & roles

Billing Rep (external · Billing Portal)

Works the AR queue daily: payer calls, appeals, touch logging, practice requests. Sees only claims for practices assigned to them by their manager. E.g. Angela Mensah, Bell Medics.

Billing Manager (external · Billing Portal)

Everything a rep can do, plus: team management (invite/deactivate reps, assign reps to practices), performance view across reps, and company settings. Owns the weekly report relationship with each practice.

Operations Admin (internal · Operations Admin Portal)

Onboards billing companies, assigns them to medical practices, suspends access, monitors activity. Mirrors their existing Medical Practices responsibilities.

Platform Admin (internal · Platform Admin Portal)

Platform-wide oversight: billing roles appear in Role Distribution, billing portal activity in Portal Activity, billing events in the Audit Log, billing portal defaults in Portal Configuration.

Tenancy rule (applies everywhere): every billing-portal query is scoped by billing company AND selected practice. A rep additionally sees "My accounts" within that scope. Practice staff never appear in the Billing Portal; billing users never appear in clinical portals.

One addition to the standard shell: a practice switcher in the top bar (pattern borrowed from Provider/Clerk "Current working facility"), showing the practices this billing company is contracted for. All content reloads in that practice's scope.

5 · Billing Portal — screen by screen

5.1 Dashboard EXISTS — re-home

The current Billing Home, re-scoped: greeting ("Hi, Angela!"), today's numbers (decisions waiting, follow-ups due, claims in flight, collected MTD), "Needs you now" list, workspace cards. New: numbers reflect the selected practice; a manager sees an additional per-rep workload strip (each rep's due-today count) for the selected practice.

5.2 Agent Inbox EXISTS — re-home

Unchanged functionally: pipeline stages, decision cards, approvals, prevention cross-links. Scoped to the selected practice. The two demo stubs ("Edit first", "Escalate") remain flagged in the QA checklist for a future decision.

5.3 AR Worklist EXISTS — re-home

Unchanged functionally: priority queue (P1–P4, filing deadlines, low-balance write-off policy), focused work view, voice/quick/typed capture, agent-polished notes, smart practice requests. Scoped to the selected practice; "My accounts" scopes to the signed-in rep.

5.4 Claims EXISTS — re-home

Claim detail (timeline, versions, payer responses, payments, source-note modal) unchanged. Known prototype boundary stays: one sample claim page. A claims list landing (search + filters → detail) is specified but deferred to Phase 2 (§9) — the worklist is the primary entry into claims.

5.5 Analytics EXISTS — re-home

Unchanged functionally: KPIs vs MGMA/HFMA benchmarks, aging, payer performance, denial prevention. Scoped per practice; shared-with-practice framing stays.

5.6 Settings NEW

Team tab (manager only): roster table (name, role Rep/Manager, assigned practices, status, last active), Invite Rep (email + role + practice assignments), deactivate/reactivate, per-rep due-today load. Company profile tab: company name, contact, logo slot. Notifications tab: per-user toggles — SLA breaches, appeal outcomes, practice replies, weekly report ready. Reps see only Notifications + their own profile; the Team tab is the visible role difference.

6 · Billing administration — in Operations Admin

Not a separate portal: billing companies are governed from Operations Admin, exactly where medical practices are governed today. A new Billing Companies nav item (People group) with three screens:

6.1 Billing Companies — list NEW

Table: company, status (Active / Onboarding / Suspended), practices served, users, open AR under management, last activity. Primary action: "Onboard Billing Company". Row → company detail.

6.2 Company detail NEW

Four tabs. Overview: profile + health tiles (open AR, touches/week, collected MTD, SLA status). Practice assignments: the many-to-many control — assign/unassign practices, effective dates, per-assignment status; unassigning revokes that practice's data instantly. Users: read-only roster of the company's team (managed by the company's manager, visible for support/audit; ops can force-deactivate). Activity: audit trail of company-level events (assignment changes, suspensions, manager invites).

6.3 Onboarding wizard NEW

Three steps, matching the existing onboarding pattern: (1) company profile, (2) assign practice(s), (3) invite the first Billing Manager. Ends with a summary + "company is live" state; the manager then builds their own team from Billing Portal Settings. Ops Dashboard gains one tile: "Billing Companies · N active" beside Medical Practices.

7 · Platform Admin integration

  • Role Distribution gains two roles: Billing Manager, Billing Rep (donut + legend counts).
  • Portal Activity gains a "Billing activity" series alongside Provider/Scribe/Clerk.
  • Audit Log ingests billing events (approvals, appeals filed, write-off requests, assignment changes) tagged with billing company + practice.
  • Portal Configuration gains a Billing Portal section: platform defaults for SLA windows, low-balance write-off threshold (default $25), quick-log outcome set, and benchmark targets.
  • KPI tiles: "Platform Users" counts include billing users, with the subtitle updated accordingly.

No new Platform Admin screens — billing becomes another population inside the existing oversight surfaces.

8 · Entities & scoping model

BillingCompany { id, name, status, contact }
PracticeAssignment { billingCompanyId, practiceId, status, effectiveDate }  // many-to-many
BillingUser { id, companyId, role: manager|rep, practiceIds[], status }
Claim / Touch / PracticeRequest / Appeal … all carry { practiceId } and actions carry { billingUserId }
  • Billing Portal reads: filtered by company's active PracticeAssignments ∩ user's practiceIds ∩ selected practice.
  • Practice-side surfaces (weekly report, practice requests, shared Analytics) show the billing company's name on everything it produced.
  • Suspending a company or unassigning a practice removes access immediately; historical actions remain in the practice's record and audit log.

9 · Build phases

  • Phase 1 — Billing Portal shell + re-home. Portal shell (top bar "Billing Portal", practice switcher, billing-only nav); the five existing screens re-homed into it; internal nav items removed. Largest visual change, lowest functional risk.
  • Phase 2 — Settings & team. Team roster, invite flow, role difference (manager vs rep), company profile, notification preferences. Optional: claims list landing.
  • Phase 3 — Operations Admin: Billing Companies. List, company detail (4 tabs), onboarding wizard, dashboard tile.
  • Phase 4 — Platform Admin touches. Roles, activity series, audit entries, portal configuration section.

Each phase follows the established loop: detailed plan → your approval → implementation → embedded QA (automated pass + NEW steps appended to the QA Checklist) → handoff.

10 · Open questions

  • Clerk Portal residue: do internal clerks keep a read-only "Billing status" view, or does billing disappear from the Clerk Portal entirely?
  • Practice-side counterpart: where does the practice answer practice requests and read the weekly report — Clerk Portal inbox, Provider Portal, or a Practice Admin surface?
  • Rep-to-practice assignment granularity: can one rep serve multiple practices (assumed yes) and does the manager assign per-practice or per-payer books of business?
  • Demo stubs: "Edit first" / "Escalate" in the Agent Inbox still need a build-or-boundary decision.
