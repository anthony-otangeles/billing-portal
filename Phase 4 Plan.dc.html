Phase 4a — Ops Portal Ripple Effects

Billing companies become a first-class tenant everywhere the Ops portal already touches people and organizations — Users, Onboarding, Medical Practices, Analytics, Audit Log, Notifications — with hard tenant isolation as the governing rule. I recommend splitting Phase 4: 4a (this plan) = the ripples you confirmed; 4b (next) = the PRD's platform-admin touches (portal configuration, role definitions). One phase would be too large to review well.

1 · Governing rule — tenant isolation

No cross-contamination — patient or company

Per your note, the future is multiple billing companies serving different practices. Every screen in this phase is built to demonstrate the boundary:

  • A billing company's users, claims, AR, and settings are visible only inside its own portal and to Operations — never to another billing company or to practices it doesn't serve.
  • Ops screens show no PHI: patient names never appear in Ops Users, Analytics, Audit Log, or company pages — only aggregates and masked references (matching your masked-email convention).
  • Practice detail shows only the name of its billing company and contract facts — no other company's data.
  • The Audit Log + "Viewing as Operations" banner make Ops access itself visible and accountable.
