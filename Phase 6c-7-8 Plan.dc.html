Phases 6c · 7 · 8 — Detailed Build Plans

The three planned gaps from the Capability Assessment, taken from one-paragraph outlines to full screen-and-workflow specs. Each phase ships real screens in this prototype plus the product features behind them (agent behaviors, decision types, store extensions), following the same supervision model as everything already built. Build order: 6c → 7 → 8 — 7 must exist before 8 can trigger off a posted primary 835.

PHASE 6C

Practice Window

effort M · builds first

The practice — the paying customer — finally sees the work. The weekly report becomes a live view, and the "billing needs from you" email thread becomes an in-product queue with uploads. This phase also carries the first slice of real documents & attachments, because the practice window is where most documents enter the system.

Screen 1 — Practice Billing View Practice Billing View.dc.html · new
  • Header strip: collected MTD vs same point last month, claims in flight, balance by aging bucket, denials prevented this month — the weekly report's four headline numbers, live.
  • Needs-from-you queue (the core): every Coverage exception whose needs line points at the practice — updated insurance card, COB confirmation, demographic fix, redetermination assist — as actionable cards with due date, dollar at risk, and an upload / mark done / can't provide flow.
  • Report archive: the weekly reports the AR Worklist already generates, listed by week, viewable in place.
  • Claims for this practice: read-only, simplified status language (no payer jargon) — "with insurance," "needs your help," "paid."
  • Strictly read-only outside the queue: no approvals, no agent supervision, no other tenants visible.
Feature — Documents & attachments, slice 1 documents-store.js · new shared store
  • A document record: type (insurance card, COB form, appeal attachment, EOB, contract), linked entity (patient / claim / exception), uploader, timestamp, preview.
  • Practice uploads via the needs-queue land as documents attached to the exception; the answered exception flows back to the Agent Inbox as the existing "practice replied" decision — now with the actual attachment on it.
  • Billing-side surfaces read the same store: Claim Detail gains a Documents section; AR Worklist appeal drafts show their attachments for real.
Placement decision (from the Phase 6 plan, still open): scoped as a standalone practice-facing screen reachable from the practice switcher, styled as its own lightweight portal — not a Clerk Portal tab. Rationale: the audience is the practice administrator, not the clerk persona, and a separate surface keeps the tenancy story clean. Say the word if you want it inside the Clerk Portal instead.
Cross-screen edits: Coverage exceptions gain "sent to practice" status; Agent Inbox "practice replied" decisions carry attachments; Billing Home follow-ups link into the queue; Ops Audit Log records practice-side actions.
Demo story unlocked: a termed-coverage exception raised on the Coverage board → appears in the practice queue → practice uploads the new card → decision closes in the Inbox with the document attached — the full loop, no email.
PHASE 7A

Contracts & Fee Schedules

effort M · builds between 6c and 7 · decisions locked Jul 13

The rate data everything downstream consumes: expected-payment forecasting per visit, Coverage's revenue-at-risk, Analytics yield per payer, and Phase 7's underpayment detection. Decisions locked with you: commercial contracts load as flat per-CPT exhibits; inferred rates are flag-only (never grounds for an appeal); the billing company maintains contracts, visible read-only in Ops.

Screen — Contracts & Fee Schedules Contracts.dc.html · new top-level
  • Contract list per payer: effective dates, timely-filing limit, rate basis (exhibit loaded / public schedule / inferred from payments), coverage of the practice's CPT mix ("38 of 41 billed codes have a contracted rate").
  • Exhibit upload: per-CPT rate table loaded from CSV or keyed in; versioned by effective date. Medicare/Medicaid ship pre-loaded as public reference schedules.
  • Rate lookup: type a CPT, see expected allowed across payers side by side — the forecasting answer at a glance.
  • Provenance + drift: per rate, the recent payments that validated or contradicted it. When payments consistently disagree, the agent proposes a rate review — a decision in the Inbox, never an automatic change.
Feature — Expected-payment engine contracts-store.js · new shared store
  • Lookup: payer × CPT × effective date → expected allowed, tagged CONTRACTED or INFERRED. Inferred rates come from paid-claims history and never justify an appeal — flag-only.
  • Consumers: Agent Inbox shows expected payment at claim generation; Coverage revenue-at-risk becomes CPT-mix × expected allowed; Analytics adds contracted-vs-collected yield per payer; Phase 7 variance queue gets its "expected" side.
  • Ownership: billing company edits (manager role); Ops sees contracts read-only on Company Detail; changes land in the Audit Log.
Demo story unlocked: open Contracts, look up 99309 across payers → see MHS HIP paying 89% of its loaded rate over the last 30 payments → agent has flagged the drift → approve the rate review, and the underpayment population is queued for Phase 7's variance work.
PHASE 7

Payment Posting & Reconciliation

effort L · builds second

The daily bread. The agent posts clean remits automatically; humans see deposits reconcile and work only the variances — the same supervise-by-exception model, applied to cash. The portal stops saying "835 received" and starts saying "posted, balanced, banked."

Screen — Payments Payments.dc.html · new top-level, 3 tabs
  • Tab 1 · Deposits: ERA batches matched to bank deposits — date, payer, check/EFT trace, ERA total vs bank total, matched / unmatched / partial. Agent auto-matches on trace number; unmatched items become worklist rows. A day is "reconciled" when every deposit ties out.
  • Tab 2 · Posting runs: each 835's line-level posting — paid, contractual adjustment (CARC/RARC coded), transferred to secondary/patient — with the agent's automation rate per run. Clean lines post at Tier 3 (autonomous, QA-sampled); exceptions drop to Tab 3.
  • Tab 3 · Variance queue: paid ≠ expected. Categories: underpayment vs contracted rate, takeback/recoupment, interest, duplicate, zero-pay with patient-responsibility shift. Agent proposes a disposition — post as-is / appeal underpayment / request reprocess / write off — with evidence; manager approves above threshold.
Feature — Write-off governance
  • Per-company thresholds in Settings (e.g. rep approves ≤$50, manager ≤$500, above that dual sign-off); Ops Portal Config can cap the ceiling platform-wide.
  • Every write-off carries reason code + approver and lands in the Audit Log and Analytics adjustments rollup.
Feature — Ledger becomes real claims-store.js extension
  • Per-claim transaction ledger: charge → payments → adjustments → transfers → balance, always summing. Claim Detail's Payments section renders it; this ledger is the substrate Phase 8 extends.
  • New Agent Inbox decision types: variance disposition, write-off request, unmatched deposit. New Analytics tiles: posting automation rate, days deposit-to-posted, net collection rate (now computable for real).
Dependency resolved: the variance queue's "expected" side comes from Phase 7a's expected-payment engine — CONTRACTED rates can drive appeal/reprocess proposals; INFERRED rates only flag for human review.
Demo story unlocked: Tuesday's Medicare deposit lands → 96% of lines auto-post → three variances queue up → agent flags one as a $41/visit underpayment against the contracted rate across 12 claims → manager approves the reprocess request batch → net collection rate ticks up in Analytics.
PHASE 8

Secondary Billing & Patient Responsibility

effort L · builds third (needs Phase 7 ledger)

A balance is owned until it is zero. When the primary 835 posts, the remaining balance doesn't vanish — it moves to the next payer in the COB stack (which Coverage already maintains) or to the patient, and stays visible and worked the whole way.

Feature — Balance lifecycle extends the Phase 7 ledger · no new top-level screen
  • Balance owner as a first-class state: PRIMARY → SECONDARY → TERTIARY → PATIENT → ZERO, each transition on the ledger with its trigger (835 posted, crossover confirmed, secondary EOB, statement sent).
  • Crossover detection: when the primary indicates auto-crossover (Medicare → Medigap), the agent tracks it and only intervenes if no secondary response arrives in N days. Otherwise the agent generates the secondary claim from the primary 835 + COB stack — a new pipeline stage in the Agent Inbox, tier-gated like primary generation.
  • Patient responsibility: after the last payer, balance moves to PATIENT with a statement status (pending / sent / paid / plan / uncollectible-requested). Statement generation itself is the vendor decision (§Decisions) — the state machine ships regardless, so the portal never loses the balance.
Screen extensions
  • Claims Register: balance-owner column + filter ("show me everything sitting with secondary payers >30 days").
  • Claim Detail: timeline extends through secondary adjudication; ledger shows the full primary → secondary → patient movement.
  • AR Worklist: secondary denials and stalled crossovers enter the same priority queue with COB-specific call prep.
  • Analytics + Practice Window: aging split by balance owner; the practice sees "with patient" balances honestly for the first time.
Demo story unlocked: the AR report's 409 "moved to secondary/patient" claims ($17.0K) — today invisible — become a filterable population: crossovers tracked, agent-generated secondary claims in the pipeline, patient balances with statement status.
